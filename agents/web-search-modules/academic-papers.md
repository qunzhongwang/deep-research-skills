# Academic Papers Module

> Search strategies for finding academic papers, surveys, and research literature.

**Trigger scenarios**: Paper search, academic research, algorithm fundamentals

## Sources
- **Google Scholar** (scholar.google.com) — comprehensive academic search
- **arXiv** (arxiv.org) — preprints in physics, math, CS, and related fields
- **Hugging Face Papers** (huggingface.co/papers) — trending ML/AI papers with community votes
- **bioRxiv** (biorxiv.org) — biology and life sciences preprints
- **ResearchGate** (researchgate.net) — academic social network with papers and profiles
- **Semantic Scholar** (semanticscholar.org) — AI-powered academic search
- **ACM Digital Library** and **IEEE Xplore** — CS and engineering papers

## Query Strategies
- Use Google Scholar as the primary source with advanced search operators
- Search by author, title, DOI, institution, and publication year
- Use quotation marks for exact titles and author name combinations
- Include year ranges to find both seminal works and recent publications
- Follow citation chains to identify foundational papers
- Search for preprints on arXiv, bioRxiv, and institutional repositories
- Check author profiles and ResearchGate for publications and PDFs
- Look for open-access versions and legal download sources
- Track citation networks to understand research evolution
- Note impact factors, h-index, and citation counts for relevance
- Search conference proceedings, journals, and workshop papers
